小学生英语单词重音产出研究（A Study of English Word Stress Production by Primary School Students）

发表于 2019-05-06 | 更新于 2019-05-08 | 分类于 毕业论文

introduction

background and necessity

In China, which is advancing with the times, English is in the compulsory courses of the majority of primary schools. A good English user is not only limited to the ability to use English for information exchange but also requires standard pronunciation. Standard English pronunciation can not only help the speaker to convey the language information but also create an emotional mood in the dialogue. From the perspective of English subject teaching, systematic articulation knowledge can help students to combine words according to pronunciation rules, and effectively reduce students’ hearing difficulties.

Most Chinese students learn English in primary school, thus making primary school a critical period of acquiring English. However, the actual oral pronunciation of Chinese primary school students is not optimistic. As a syllable-timed language, the stress assignment rules and long syllables in Chinese are more regular than in English, while the sentence stress assignment rules in English are more complicated and free. What is worrying is that Chinese primary school students cannot get rid of the negative transfer of their mother tongue when they are learning English stress. They tend to re-read each syllable without selectivity. Strong reading exists whether in a word or a sentence. In other words, the English pronunciation learning of Chinese primary school students has little priority. The duration of the syllables is the same, and the rhythm is single. Therefore, studying the pronunciation of English stress in Chinese primary school students is a crucial and fundamental step to improve the oral expression of Chinese primary school students.

purpose

structure of the thesis

The experiment carried out by Li Junmin reveals that English learners from China possess the metalinguistic knowledge, including stress assignment and stress accuracy. Meanwhile, this knowledge is significantly related to English proficiency.[1] Guo Li discovered that the current junior high school students’ English rhythm bias is serious, and it has a very significant impact on the intelligibility of the discourse. Strengthening the teaching of junior high school English rhythm is a top priority.[2]

Guo Xingrong undertakes a study of prosodic transfer effects in the production of English lexical stress by Chinese learners. The researchers examined the acoustic characteristics of pitch, duration, intensity and vowel quality in the bisyllabic words produced by native American English, Beijing Dialect and Cantonese speakers. And the results conform to the Stress Typology Model and the Prosodic Transfer Hypothesis.[3]

By comparing the beginners and advanced Chinese ESL learners with native American English speakers in terms of the acoustic characteristics of English lexical stress, Chen Xiaoxiang points out the effects of Chinese learners’ L2 proficiency on the production of English lexical stress.[4]
